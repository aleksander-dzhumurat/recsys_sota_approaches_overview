# RecSys '25

---

## Keynotes

### Recommending in the Age of AI: How We Got Here and What Comes Next

**Xavier "Xavi" Amatriain** — VP Product, AI and Compute Enablement at Google

For the recommender systems community, the current AI revolution driven by Generative AI presents both an existential challenge and an unprecedented opportunity. This keynote provides a historical and future-looking perspective, reflecting on the Netflix Prize era, distinguishing enduring principles of recommendation from new rules written by today's AI models, and discussing the implications of auto-generated personalized media and multi-agentic recommender systems.

---

### Recommender Systems: A European, Science for Policy Perspective

**Emilia Gómez** — Senior Scientist, European Commission's Joint Research Center

This keynote explores the policy perspective on recommender systems, including the EU Digital Services Act and AI Act through the lens of algorithmic transparency. It presents mechanisms to integrate scientific, technical, and policy perspectives, drawing from work at the European Centre for Algorithmic Transparency.

---

### Relational Foundation Models: A New Frontier for Predictive AI in Structured Data

**Jure Leskovec** — Professor of Computer Science, Stanford University

This keynote introduces Relational Foundation Models (RFMs), a new class of pre-trained models that unlock in-context learning over relational data — including transaction logs, customer journeys, events, and time series — without per-task supervision or feature engineering, reshaping the paradigm of predictive AI.

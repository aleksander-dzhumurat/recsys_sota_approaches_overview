### Main Idea
- The paper addresses the cold start problem in sequential recommender systems, where new items with few or no interactions cannot be effectively recommended.
- The motivation is to improve recommendation quality for cold items without sacrificing performance on warm items and to increase recommendation diversity and model lifetime.
- The proposed solution involves using content-based embeddings (e.g., from text descriptions or audio features) for items and then adding a small, trainable "delta" vector to these embeddings. This allows the model to adapt the item representations slightly without deviating too far from the original content-based meaning. The norm of the delta vector is constrained by clipping at a maximum value.

### Technologies
- **Model:** SASRec (Self-Attentive Sequential Recommendation) is used as the base sequential recommendation model.
- **Content Embeddings:** Text embeddings are generated using the E5 encoder. Precomputed audio embeddings are used for the Zvuk dataset.
- **Dimensionality Reduction:** Principal Component Analysis (PCA) is used to reduce the dimensionality of content embeddings to match the model's embedding dimension. Component-wise standardization is applied before PCA.
- **Optimization:** Adam optimizer with a learning rate of 1e-3. Full cross-entropy loss is used.
- **Evaluation Metrics:** Normalized Discounted Cumulative Gain (NDCG@10) and Hit Rate (HR@10) are used to evaluate recommendation quality. The performance is evaluated separately for cold and warm items.
- **Datasets:** Amazon-M2 (e-commerce with text descriptions), Beauty (e-commerce with text), and Zvuk (music streaming with audio embeddings).
- **Implementation:** Experiments are performed using the Replay library and custom code available on GitHub.

### Results
- The proposed method (SASRec with trainable delta) consistently improves performance on cold items across all three datasets compared to using content-based KNN or standard SASRec.
- It also maintains or slightly improves performance on warm items, indicating that the approach doesn't degrade recommendations for items with sufficient interaction data.
- Analysis of the delta vector norm (𝛿 max) shows that an intermediate value (0.3 to 0.6) provides a good trade-off between adapting item representations and preserving the original content-based information.
- Using content-based initialization significantly improves recommendation quality when cold items are present in the input sequence.
- The trainable delta approach demonstrates improvements for rare items.
- The main limitation mentioned is the additional memory overhead due to maintaining two embedding vectors per item. Future work could focus on reducing embedding size or exploring other modalities and scenarios.

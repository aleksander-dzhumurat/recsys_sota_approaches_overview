### Main Idea
- This paper presents a systematic literature review (SLR) analyzing current trends, challenges, and the effectiveness of various machine learning (ML) methods in e-commerce recommendation systems (RSs).
- The motivation is to understand how these systems enhance user experience and drive sales in e-commerce, given the increasing reliance on them. The significance lies in providing actionable insights for improving the design and efficiency of RSs.
- The approach involves conducting an SLR of 38 publications from 2013 to 2025, evaluating and comparing ML methods such as collaborative filtering, content-based filtering, and hybrid models to determine their performance and effectiveness in addressing e-commerce challenges. The research questions are centered around the effectiveness of ML methods, challenges in implementation, comparison of hybrid and traditional approaches, and future trends.

### Technologies
- **Key technical components:** Collaborative filtering (CF), Content-based filtering (CBF), Hybrid models, Deep learning, Reinforcement learning.
- **Algorithms and frameworks:** CNN-based models, pre-trained language models, model-based CF.
- **Datasets:** Not specified, but the paper mentions that varying datasets are used across different studies, making direct comparisons challenging.
- **Evaluation metrics:** Accuracy, precision, recall, F1-score, MAE, RMSE are used to assess performance.
- **Experimental setup:** Systematic Literature Review (SLR) conducted across IEEE Xplore, ScienceDirect, and ACM Digital Library, using specific inclusion and exclusion criteria. The search query used was: ("recommendation systems" OR "recommender systems") AND ("e-commerce" OR "online shopping") AND ("machine learning" OR "deep learning" OR "reinforcement learning" OR "hybrid methods" OR "collaborative filtering").
- **Novel technical contributions:** Identification of effective solutions for challenges like cold start problems, data sparsity, and balancing accuracy with diversity. The paper also highlights the increasing use of hybrid methods and emerging techniques like Deep Learning and Reinforcement Learning.

### Results
- Collaborative filtering (CF), content-based filtering (CBF), and hybrid methods are the most commonly used ML techniques in e-commerce recommendation systems.
- Hybrid models outperform traditional methods in handling diverse user needs and complex datasets.
- Deep learning and reinforcement learning are gaining traction for improved scalability, personalization, and adaptability.
- The paper highlights that hybrid models are effective in addressing challenges like the cold start problem, data sparsity, and overfitting. CNN-based models are used to filter out unhelpful reviews.
- Performance metrics vary across studies due to different datasets, making direct comparisons challenging, although insights into the relative strengths of various machine learning techniques can be gleaned from these comparisons.
- The study indicates that advancements in hybrid and deep learning-based approaches are needed to address the limitations in current recommendation systems. Scalability and addressing the cold-start problem remain significant challenges for large-scale systems.

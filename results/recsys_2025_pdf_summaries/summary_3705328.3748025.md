### Main Idea
- The paper addresses the problem of how to effectively guide users towards developing new musical tastes based on their current preferences, specifically in scenarios where target genres differ significantly from existing tastes.
- It argues that current exploration approaches, which often suggest representative songs directly from target genres, can be overwhelming. The core idea is to use incremental, feedback-driven recommendations to gradually introduce users to unfamiliar music genres. This aims to balance exploration and exploitation, maintaining user engagement while expanding their musical horizons.
- The proposed solution is an adaptive learning approach that refines recommendation paths based on user feedback, dynamically balancing the exploration-exploitation tradeoff.

### Technologies
- **Collaborative Knowledge Graph (CKG)**: Fuses professionally curated metadata (genres, playlists), implicit user behavior (likes, plays), and audio similarity. Song-to-song edges are derived from MULE embeddings, weighted by cosine similarity.
- **Node2vec**: Used to embed the CKG into a vector space, capturing nuanced relationships between songs.
- **K-Nearest Neighbor (KNN) Graph**: Constructed to maintain the integrity of original representations while enabling structured navigation through a high-dimensional space, connecting each song to its 10 closest neighbors.
- **PageRank**: Algorithm used to identify central nodes representing subgroups within user preferences and target genres, defining starting and target points for exploration paths.
- **Breadth-First Search**: Used to generate standardized paths between starting and target points.
- **Bayesian Active Learning**: Employs Thompson Sampling (TS) to dynamically balance exploration and exploitation of paths, maintaining a belief state (Beta prior) over each path's utility. Beliefs are updated based on user feedback (likes/dislikes), weighted by cosine similarity between songs in different paths to improve sample efficiency.
- **User Study**: Online experiment with three conditions (Big Step, Small Steps Passive, Small Steps Active) to evaluate the approach's effectiveness.
- **Evaluation Metrics**: Total likes, target genre likes, perceived helpfulness, affinity toward the target genre, perceived control, system understandability, quality of direction, and perceived personalization.

### Results
- The Small Steps Active (SSA) method, which incorporates Bayesian active learning, significantly increased user engagement compared to the Big Step (BS) approach, with users liking substantially more songs overall (44.80 vs. 14.33) and showing a higher affinity towards the target genre (0.34 vs. 0.12).
- Users rated gradual approaches (Small Steps Passive + Small Steps Active) significantly higher than the baseline on subjective measures like perceived helpfulness (5.51 vs. 3.39), quality of direction, personalization, control, and understandability.
- Final affinity toward the target genre was substantially higher in the gradual approaches compared to the baseline (4.87 vs. 1.94), with personalization serving as a significant mediator.
- The study provides preliminary support for the hypotheses that gradual exploration is more effective than direct genre introduction (H1) and that feedback-driven adaptation further enhances these benefits (H2).
- Limitations include a limited sample size, the absence of a control group receiving general recommendations, and the experimental complexity. Future work should address these limitations and extend the approach to other content domains.

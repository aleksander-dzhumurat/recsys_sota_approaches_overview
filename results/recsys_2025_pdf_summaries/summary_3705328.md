### Main Idea
- The paper addresses the challenge of improving the accuracy of medication recommendations made by Large Language Models (LLMs) in clinical decision support systems. LLMs often lack specialized medical knowledge and exposure to real-world patient data, limiting their effectiveness in this critical task.
- The authors introduce TreatRAG, a novel, model-agnostic, Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) framework designed for early-stage development to enhance medication recommendation accuracy. The core idea is to leverage publicly available clinical data to augment LLMs' capabilities without requiring additional training. This forms a foundational step towards clinical validation and domain expert involvement in the future.
- TreatRAG enhances LLM predictions by retrieving similar patient cases (digital twins) using interpretable N-gram Jaccard similarity and incorporating them into the input prompt, grounding the LLM's predictions in real clinical scenarios.

### Technologies
- **Framework:** TreatRAG, a retrieval-augmented generation framework.
- **LLMs:** BioGPT, BioMistral, Phi3, and Flan-T5 are used as the base language models to be augmented.
- **Dataset:** MIMIC-IV, a comprehensive clinical dataset containing patient records from the Intensive Care Unit (ICU), including patient admission, diagnoses, and prescription data.
- **Similarity Metric:** N-gram Jaccard similarity is used to retrieve similar patient cases. This method compares patient history and diagnoses with past records based on token-level overlap, making it interpretable. Gap statistics are used to filter out less informative matches.
- **Prompt Engineering:** Structured clinical inputs are formatted into natural language to enhance model compatibility. Patient records are preprocessed into structured textual prompts, summarizing each visit with diagnoses and prescribed medications.
- **Evaluation Metrics:** F1-score and Jaccard similarity are used to evaluate the performance of the models. F1-score provides a balanced measure of precision and recall for the predicted ATC codes, while Jaccard similarity quantifies the overlap between predicted and ground-truth medication sets. A hallucination analysis is performed by comparing predicted drug terms against valid ATC-3 codes.
- **Implementation details:** The framework is optimized to utilize available GPUs for fast and accurate similarity computation across large datasets. Adaptive retrieval is performed, selecting up to k similar cases, typically fewer than 6 after filtering by adaptive thresholds (mean + 1.5 × standard deviation). Truncation strategies are applied to meet the model's token limitations.

### Results
- TreatRAG significantly improves the performance of all evaluated LLMs in medication prediction.
- TreatRAG-enhanced BioGPT improves its F1-score from 0.14 to 0.34, BioMistral from 0.22 to 0.54, Phi-3 from 0.09 to 0.16, and Flan-T5 from 0.23 to 0.30.
- The framework also significantly lowers the hallucination rate of the LLMs. For example, TreatRAG reduces hallucination rates for BioGPT from 84.41% to 39.85% and for BioMistral from 72.83% to 32.76%.
- Ablation studies show that N-gram Jaccard similarity outperforms TF-IDF-based cosine similarity and dense vector retrieval across all models.
- TreatRAG consistently outperforms baseline models across all rarity thresholds of diseases, with particularly strong improvements under the rarest settings (&lt;50 patients). BioGPT's F1-score more than triples, from 0.10 to 0.33, and BioMistral's Jaccard similarity improves from 0.22 to 0.35 in this low-resource setting.
- The results demonstrate that TreatRAG is a flexible, effective, and interpretable solution for improving the reliability of LLMs in clinical decision support, especially in data-sparse environments.

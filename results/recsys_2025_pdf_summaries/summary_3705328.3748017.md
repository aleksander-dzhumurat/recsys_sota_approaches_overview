### Main Idea

- **Primary Research Objective:** The paper addresses the problem of popularity bias in Point-of-Interest (POI) recommender systems, where popular locations dominate recommendations, disadvantaging less popular yet potentially meaningful places.
- **Motivation and Significance:** Popularity bias leads to unfair treatment of both item providers (niche locations) and consumers (users preferring less mainstream locations), potentially causing overcrowding, environmental degradation, and reduced user satisfaction.
- **Proposed Approach:** The research systematically evaluates the individual and combined effects of context-aware models (LORE and USG) and calibrated popularity (CP) techniques on recommendation accuracy and popularity bias using real-world POI datasets. The goal is to determine if these strategies complement each other and how they jointly affect the trade-off between accuracy and popularity bias across different user groups.

### Technologies

- **Models/Algorithms:**
    - **Bayesian Personalized Ranking (BPR):** A non-contextualized baseline recommendation model.
    - **LOcation REcommendation approach (LORE):** A context-aware model integrating sequential, social, and geographical influences using additive Markov chains and a location-location transition graph.
    - **U ser preference, S ocial influence, and G eographical influence (USG):** A hybrid context-aware model combining user- and friend-based collaborative filtering with geographical influence using a naive Bayes approach. Note: social influence was not used due to data constraints.
    - **Calibrated Popularity (CP):** A re-ranking method that adjusts a base recommendation list to better reflect a user's historical preferences for item popularity levels.  Two variations were tested: 𝐶𝑃 𝐻 (optimizing the harmonic mean of accuracy and calibration) and 𝐶𝑃 ℑ (minimizing Jensen-Shannon divergence, prioritizing calibration).
- **Datasets:**
    - Brightkite
    - Foursquare
    - Gowalla
    - Yelp
- **Evaluation Metrics:**
    - **nDCG (Normalized Discounted Cumulative Gain):** Measures recommendation accuracy.
    - **ARP (Average Recommendation Popularity):** Illustrates the popularity of the recommendations.
    - **PopLift (Popularity Lift):** Reflects whether the popularity of the recommendations is higher, lower, or close to the popularity of items in the user profile for each user group.
    - **Jensen-Shannon divergence (ℑ):** Measures the distributional similarity between the user's historical popularity distribution and the recommendation list's distribution.
- **Experimental Setup:**
    - Users are grouped by their average profile popularity into LowPop, MedPop, and HighPop groups.
    - Items are classified as tail (T), mid (M), and head (H) based on popularity.
    - A user-based temporal split is applied to create training (65%), validation (15%), and test (20% most recent check-ins) sets.
    - Models are trained for 200 epochs, and the top-150 recommendations per user are stored for CP-based re-ranking to evaluate the final top-10 recommendations.
- **Tools/Frameworks:**
    - **CAPRI framework:** Used to implement the context-aware models (LORE and USG).
    - **RecBole:** Used to generate baseline recommendations (BPR).

### Results

- **RQ1 (Individual Impact):**
    - LORE effectively reduces popularity bias by recommending niche locations but often at the cost of lower accuracy. In Yelp dataset, it improved accuracy for LowPop users.
    - USG increases popularity bias in Foursquare, recommending almost exclusively popular items and decreasing accuracy, while showing better results on Yelp with reduced popularity bias and non-significant accuracy increase.
    - 𝐶𝑃 𝐻 (accuracy-oriented calibration) leads to a significant decrease in ARP and PopLift for most user groups without significantly impacting accuracy.
    - 𝐶𝑃 ℑ (calibration-oriented) leads to significant decreases in both accuracy and popularity bias across the board.
- **RQ2 (Combined Impact):**
    - Applying CP to LORE improves accuracy but increases the ARP of the recommended items.  It aligns the popularity distribution of the recommendations more closely with the user profile.
    - Combining USG with 𝐶𝑃 𝐻 shows no significant impact on nDCG, but the combination of USG and 𝐶𝑃 ℑ consistently reduces ARP and PopLift across all user groups, indicating popularity bias mitigation.
- **Key Insights:**
    - The effectiveness of context-awareness varies substantially between models.
    - The effectiveness of CP depends on the chosen 𝜆 value (balancing accuracy and calibration) and varies for different user groups.
    - Combining context-awareness and CP helps counteract the shortcomings of both methods when applied individually.
    - If mitigating popularity bias is the primary objective, combining LORE with CP is recommended.
    - If preserving accuracy while slightly reducing bias is preferred, general models such as BPR or context-aware models like USG combined with CP and tuned towards accuracy (e.g., 𝐶𝑃 𝐻 ) are more suitable.
- **Limitations and Future Work:**
    - The authors plan to conduct user studies to validate the findings and assess the impact on user satisfaction.
    - They also plan to investigate the effects on other RS stakeholders, such as POI providers.

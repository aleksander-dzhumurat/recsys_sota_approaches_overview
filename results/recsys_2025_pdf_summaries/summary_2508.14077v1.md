### Main Idea
- The paper revisits label smoothing, a technique that softens one-hot target vectors in machine learning, through the lens of the Information Bottleneck (IB) principle.
- The core problem is to understand the behavior and effects of label smoothing, particularly its connection to information-theoretic regularization.
- The paper proposes interpreting label smoothing as a practical implementation of the Information Bottleneck, which aims to compress information about the input while preserving information about the target. Under the assumptions of sufficient model flexibility and no conflicting labels, label smoothing explores optimal solutions of the information bottleneck.

### Technologies
- **Label Smoothing (LS)**: A technique to soften hard one-hot target vectors by averaging them with another distribution (e.g., uniform).
- **Information Bottleneck (IB)**: A framework for representation learning that aims to compress information about the input while preserving information about the target.
- **Variational Information Bottleneck (VIB)**: A practical implementation of the Information Bottleneck using variational inference to approximate mutual information.
- **Cross-Entropy Loss (CE)**: A standard loss function for classification tasks.
- **Datasets**: CIFAR-10, Flowers-102, Occluded CIFAR, and a variant of Cluttered MNIST.
- **Models**: ResNet-56, ResNet-20, Compact Convolutional Transformer (CCT-7/7x2).
- **Optimization**: SGD with momentum, Adam.
- **Evaluation**: Accuracy, mutual information, visualization of attention regions (Grad-CAM).
- **Other**: Kullback-Leibler (KL) divergence.

### Results
- **Theoretical Findings**:
    - Demonstrated a correspondence between label smoothing and Variational IB, interpreting label smoothing as a form of IB.
    - Proved that under practical assumptions (model flexibility, no conflicting labels), label smoothing can explore the optimal solutions of IB with varying compression levels.
- **Experimental Validation**:
    - Confirmed theoretically driven IB optimality for label smoothing when there are no contradicting labels, showing the solutions align with the IB curve.
    - Showed that in cases with contradicting labels (CIFAR-10H), label smoothing with a uniform smoothing distribution may not be IB-optimal, due to the fact that uniform smoothing distribution doesn't take inter-class similarity into account.
    - Demonstrated that label smoothing makes models less sensitive to nuisance factors (Occluded CIFAR) by removing information about the occluding MNIST images in the penultimate layer.
    - Visualized that label smoothing helps remove redundant factors (variant of Cluttered MNIST) by making the model focus on the original image rather than cluttered versions, visualizing via attention maps generated by Grad-CAM.

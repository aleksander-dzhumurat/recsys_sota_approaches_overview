### Main Idea

-   The paper addresses the challenge of integrating natural language interfaces into music recommendation systems. Current approaches using Large Language Models (LLMs) face scalability issues due to high costs and latency, while retrieval-based methods often rely on single-modal item representations, ignore long-term user preferences, and require full model retraining.
-   The authors propose JAM (Just Ask for Music), a lightweight framework that models user-query-item interactions as vector translations in a shared latent space, inspired by knowledge graph embedding methods. This approach aims to efficiently incorporate multimodal item features (audio, lyrics, collaborative filtering signals) and both short-term (query) and long-term (user history) user preferences.
-   JAM aggregates multimodal item features via cross-attention and sparse mixture-of-experts. The paper also introduces JAMSessions, a new dataset of over 100k user-query-item triples with anonymized user/item embeddings, combining conversational queries and user long-term preferences.

### Technologies

-   **Framework**: JAM (Just Ask for Music), a translation-based model for music recommendation.
-   **User/Item Representations**: Fixed embeddings for users and items, pre-computed from collaborative filtering, audio, and lyrics. User embeddings represent long-term preferences, while queries capture short-term intent.
-   **Query Encoder**: ModernBert-base text encoder to obtain dense representations of the queries.
-   **Translation Model**: User-query-item interactions are modeled as translations in a shared latent space, inspired by TransE (u + q ≈ t).
-   **Multimodal Aggregation Strategies**:
    *   *Averaging (AvgMixing)*: Averages different modality-specific embeddings.
    *   *Cross-Attention (CrossMixing)*: Uses cross-attention to dynamically adjust the weighting of different modalities based on the query.
    *   *Sparse Mixture of Experts (MoEMixing)*: Combines representations from different modalities, considering each item modality as an expert, using Noisy Top-K gating.
-   **Loss Function**: BPR (Bayesian Personalized Ranking) loss to maximize similarity for positive user-query-item triplets and minimize it for negative triplets.
-   **Dataset**: JAMSessions, a new dataset of over 100k user-query-item triples sampled from the search logs of a music streaming service. Includes precomputed embeddings for users and playlists based on listening history.
-   **Evaluation Metrics**: Recall and Normalized Discounted Cumulative Gain (NDCG) at cut-off thresholds of 10 and 100.
-   **Baselines**: TalkRec (Oramas et al.), Two-Tower model, Random, and Most Popular item.
-   **Implementation Details**: AdamW optimizer, cosine annealing learning rate scheduler, batch size of 512, and early stopping based on validation NDCG@10.

### Results

-   JAM variants outperform baselines in terms of Recall and NDCG, demonstrating the effectiveness of integrating natural language queries with multimodal item representations and user preferences.
-   CrossMixing, the cross-attention based multimodal aggregation strategy, achieves the best performance, followed by AvgMixing.  MoEMixing shows a drop in accuracy, suggesting that all modalities contribute valuable information.
-   Analysis of top-K activations in MoEMixing and attention weights in CrossMixing indicates that collaborative filtering signals are the most influential, potentially due to the user representations being based on CF embeddings.
-   Qualitative analysis reveals that JAM can provide personalized recommendations by translating users to different regions of the item embedding space based on their queries and preferences.  For instance, the same query can yield different recommendations for different users, and a single user can receive contextually relevant suggestions based on their query.
-   Limitations include potential issues with artist-specific requests due to data sparsity and the semantic gap between artist names and the available modalities.  Future work involves enriching user profiles with multimodal features and incorporating artist embeddings.

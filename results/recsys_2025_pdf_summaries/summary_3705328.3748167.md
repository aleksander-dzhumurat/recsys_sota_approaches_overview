### Main Idea
- The paper explores using Large Language Models (LLMs) to simulate human users for evaluating serendipity in recommender systems. Evaluating serendipity is challenging due to its subjective nature, and current approaches often rely on proxy metrics that don't accurately reflect user perceptions. The core research question is whether LLMs can bridge this gap by effectively simulating human judgment in serendipity evaluation. The motivation is to find a cost-effective and accurate alternative to user studies for evaluating serendipity, potentially transforming research in this area. The proposed approach involves a meta-evaluation framework (SerenEva) that compares LLM-based serendipity scores with actual user feedback from existing user study datasets.

### Technologies
- **LLMs:**  LLaMA2-7B, LLaMA2-13B, Qwen2.5-7B, Qwen2.5-14B, Qwen2.5-72B, and GPT-4.
- **Datasets:** Taobao Serendipity (e-commerce) and Serendipity-2018 (movie). These datasets contain user ratings of serendipity obtained through user studies.
- **Proxy Metrics:** SOG (Serendipity-Oriented Greedy), SNPR (Serendipity-oriented Next POI Recommendation), PURS (Personalized Unexpected Recommender System), and DESR (Directional and Explainable Serendipity Recommendation).
- **Prompting Strategies:** Zero-shot and few-shot prompting, constrained prompting. Auxiliary data injection through structured prompting.
- **Multi-LLM Techniques:** Score averaging ensemble strategy.
- **Evaluation Metrics:** Pearson correlation coefficient, Mean Absolute Error (MAE), and Root Mean Squared Error (RMSE).
- **Meta-evaluation Framework:** SerenEva, which measures the alignment between LLM evaluator ratings and human judgments.
- **Auxiliary data**: User data (psychological, demographic, profile data), item data (popularity, similarity), and interaction data (interaction length, type) are explored to enhance LLM comprehension of serendipity.

### Results
- Zero-shot and few-shot LLMs can achieve parity with or surpass conventional proxy metrics in serendipity evaluation. Specifically, Qwen2.5-14B and GPT-4 outperform the best proxy metrics by approximately 100% in Pearson correlation.
- Incorporating auxiliary data enhances the accuracy of LLM evaluations, although the optimal choice of auxiliary data depends on the domain. For example, user curiosity is more beneficial in the e-commerce dataset, while item popularity is more crucial for the movie dataset.
- Multi-LLM techniques, especially score averaging ensembles, can further improve evaluation performance and alignment with human judgments. An ensemble of Qwen2.5-14B, Qwen2.5-72B, and GPT-4, combined with appropriate auxiliary data, yields the best results.
- The optimal evaluation by LLMs achieves a Pearson correlation coefficient of 20.23% on the Taobao dataset and 21.51% on the Serendipity-2018 dataset when compared to user study results.
- The paper highlights the potential of LLMs as reliable and reproducible evaluators for serendipity, offering a balance between accuracy and efficiency, and discusses the limitations of LLaMA family models in processing Chinese.

### Main Idea
- The paper investigates the effectiveness of offline evaluation protocols for linear Contextual Multi-Armed Bandit (CMAB) algorithms in recommender systems.
- It challenges the common assumption that exploration strategies are beneficial in offline evaluations and reveals a counterintuitive finding: a purely greedy linear model often outperforms or matches the performance of its exploratory counterparts.
- The study argues that current offline evaluation practices are systematically biased against exploration, highlighting the need for more robust assessment methodologies.

### Technologies
- **Algorithms:** Linear CMAB algorithms including LinGreedy (ε-greedy), LinUCB (Linear Upper Confidence Bound), and LinTS (Linear Thompson Sampling) are compared against a purely greedy linear model (Lin).
- **Datasets:** Experiments were conducted on a variety of publicly available datasets commonly used in recommender systems research, including Amazon Reviews, MovieLens, RetailRocket, and Delicious.
- **Evaluation Metrics:** NDCG@20 (Normalized Discounted Cumulative Gain) is used to measure the accuracy of recommendations. Novelty is used to evaluate the diversity of recommendations.
- **Experimental Setup:** The datasets are chronologically sorted and split into training (50%) and testing (50%) sets. Item embeddings are trained using ALS (Alternating Least Squares) on the training data, and user states are computed as the average of previously consumed item embeddings. Off-policy evaluation (OPE) is performed using the Open Bandit Pipeline (OBP) with estimators such as Inverse Propensity Weighting (IPW), Direct Method (DM), and Doubly Robust (DR).
- **Hyperparameter Optimization:** Hyperparameters for the CMAB algorithms (e.g., exploration parameters) are optimized using a validation set to maximize NDCG@20.

### Results
- The purely exploitative Lin (greedy) model achieved the highest NDCG@20 in 56% of the datasets and ranked either top or second-best in 89% of all cases, often outperforming exploratory CMABs.
- Exploratory algorithms rarely surpassed the Lin baseline, and in many cases, exploration led to a decrease in NDCG@20, suggesting that offline protocols penalize exploration.
- Hyperparameter optimization consistently favored configurations that minimized exploration for LinUCB and LinGreedy. LinTS showed more variation, but higher exploration settings often resulted in poor NDCG@20 performance.
- Off-policy evaluation, using estimators like IPW and DR, did not fully mitigate the bias against exploration; the greedy model still often outperformed exploratory variants.
- The study highlights the limitations of current offline evaluation protocols in capturing the true benefits of exploration and advocates for the development of more robust evaluation methodologies, including simulation-based approaches.

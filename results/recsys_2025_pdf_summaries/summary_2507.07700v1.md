### Main Idea
- This paper reproduces and extends the work of Morris et al.'s "Vec2Text" framework, which addresses the problem of reconstructing text from its embeddings generated by a black-box encoder. The motivation is to examine the privacy risks associated with storing and transmitting text embeddings instead of raw text, especially considering the potential reconstruction of sensitive information. The paper aims to validate the original claims of Vec2Text and further explore its capabilities and limitations through targeted experiments. The core approach involves replicating Vec2Text, evaluating it in both in-domain and out-of-domain settings, and extending the study with parameter sensitivity analysis, password reconstruction, and embedding quantization as a defense mechanism.

### Technologies
- **Vec2Text Framework:** A multi-step iterative approach for reconstructing text from embeddings using an encoder-decoder model.
- **Text Encoders:** GTR-base (a T5-based transformer) and text-embeddings-ada-002 (OpenAI API).
- **Inversion Model:** T5-base checkpoint.
- **Datasets:** Natural Questions (NQ), MSMARCO, BEIR benchmark datasets (Quora, Signal1M, Climate-Fever, Robust04, BioASQ, TREC-News, ArguAna, FiQA, NFCorpus, SciFact), and Password Strength dataset.
- **Evaluation Metrics:** BLEU score, Token F1, Exact-match, Cosine similarity, and nDCG@10.
- **Defense Mechanisms:** Gaussian noise addition, Absolute Maximum Quantization and Zeropoint Quantization
- **Implementation Details:** Pytorch 2.1, Ubuntu server with NVIDIA H100 GPUs, AMD EPYC CPUs, and 768G memory.

### Results
- Successfully replicated the original key results in both in-domain and out-of-domain settings with minor discrepancies due to missing artifacts like model checkpoints and dataset splits.
- Found that Vec2Text is effective under ideal conditions but is sensitive to input sequence length, leading to performance degradation with varying text lengths.
- Demonstrated that Gaussian noise and quantization techniques can mitigate privacy risks posed by Vec2Text, with quantization offering a simpler and more widely applicable solution. Adding Gaussian noise is most effective when 𝜆 = 0 . 01
- Conducted a parameter sensitivity analysis, revealing that increasing both beam width and iterative steps improves performance, with a Pareto front guiding optimal hyperparameter selection for a given runtime.
- Evaluated Vec2Text's ability to reconstruct password-like sequences, showing successful inversion of a fraction of hard passwords by the ada-ms-128 model.
- Proposed and evaluated embedding quantization as a lightweight, noise-free defense mechanism, demonstrating that it effectively reduces the reconstruction capability of Vec2Text while preserving retrieval performance.

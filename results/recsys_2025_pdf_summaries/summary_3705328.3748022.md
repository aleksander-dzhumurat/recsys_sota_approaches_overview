### Main Idea
- The paper addresses the limitations of large language models (LLMs) in providing accurate medication recommendations due to their lack of specialized medical knowledge and exposure to real-world patient data.
- It introduces TreatRAG, a model-agnostic retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) framework, to enhance the accuracy of medication recommendations by grounding LLM predictions in real clinical scenarios using publicly available clinical data.
- The proposed solution retrieves similar patient cases ("digital twins") using interpretable N-gram Jaccard similarity and augments the input prompt to LLMs with this information.

### Technologies
- **Framework:** TreatRAG, a retrieval-augmented generation framework.
- **Retrieval Method:** N-gram Jaccard similarity for finding similar patient cases (digital twins). Gap statistics are used to filter out less informative matches. Adaptive thresholds are applied to balance retrieval precision and coverage.
- **Datasets:** MIMIC-IV, a comprehensive clinical dataset containing patient records from the ICU.
- **LLMs Used:** BioGPT, BioMistral, Phi-3, and Flan-T5.
- **Evaluation Metrics:** F1-score and Jaccard similarity to evaluate the performance of medication prediction.
- **Hallucination Analysis:** Comparison of predicted drug terms against valid ATC-3 codes from the WHO classification.
- **Prompt Engineering:** Structured clinical inputs are formatted into natural language prompts for the LLMs.

### Results
- TreatRAG-enhanced models achieved statistically significant improvements in medication prediction accuracy (F1-score) over their baselines across all evaluated architectures.
    - BioGPT: Improved from 0.14 to 0.34.
    - BioMistral: Improved from 0.23 to 0.54.
    - Phi-3: Improved from 0.09 to 0.16.
    - Flan-T5: Improved from 0.23 to 0.30.
- TreatRAG significantly reduced hallucination rates for BioGPT (84.41% to 39.85%) and BioMistral (72.83% to 32.76%).
- Ablation studies showed that N-gram Jaccard similarity outperformed TF-IDF-based cosine similarity and dense vector retrieval methods.
- TreatRAG consistently outperformed baseline models across all rarity thresholds of rare diseases, with particularly strong improvements under the rarest settings (&lt;50 patients). For example, BioGPT's F1-score more than triples, from 0.10 to 0.33, and BioMistral's Jaccard similarity improves from 0.22 to 0.35.

### Main Idea
- The paper addresses the gap in understanding the relationship between group and individual fairness in recommender systems (RSs). Previous work has evaluated these two types of fairness using different measures or objectives, making direct comparisons difficult. The paper empirically studies the relationship between group and individual fairness by comparing evaluation measures that can be used for both fairness types.
- The motivation is that recent legislations mandate responsible artificial intelligence development, making RS fairness evaluation increasingly important. It's crucial to ensure users are not systematically disadvantaged. Prior work suggests that RSs can be fair to groups but unfair to individuals or vice versa.
- The proposed approach involves a comprehensive comparison of nine existing user-side fairness evaluation measures for groups with those for individuals across three datasets and with different ways of grouping users based on their sensitive attributes. The study uses both effectiveness and fairness metrics to evaluate the performance of LLM-based recommenders (LLMRecs).

### Technologies
- **Datasets**: ML-1M (movies), JobRec (jobs), and LFM-1B (music).
- **User Grouping**: Users are grouped based on sensitive attributes like gender, age, occupation (ML-1M), degree, years of experience, major (JobRec), and gender, age, country (LFM-1B).
- **LLM-based Recommenders (LLMRecs)**: Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct, Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct, GLM-4-9B-chat, and Ministral-8B-Instruct-2410.
- **Prompting**: In-context learning (ICL) strategy, with two prompt types: Sensitive (S) which includes user sensitive attributes, and Non-Sensitive (NS) which doesn't.
- **Evaluation Metrics**: Hit Rate (HR), MRR, P@k, NDCG@k for effectiveness. Gini Index (Gini), Atkinson Index (Atk), Standard Deviation (SD), Range, MAD, CV, FStat, KL, and GCE for fairness. Kendall's 𝜏 to assess agreement in ranking models.
- **Experimental Setup**: Temporal split of data into train/val/test sets. Fuzzy string matching for evaluating LLMRecs. Experiments carried out with vllm and RecLM-eval.

### Results
- The study finds that recommendations that are highly fair for groups can be very unfair for individuals. Group fairness measures often hide unfairness within groups and between individuals.
- No individual fairness measure consistently provides equivalent rankings to any group fairness measure. Thus, individual and group fairness measures reach different conclusions.
- Fairness worsens as more attributes are used to form groups, highlighting the importance of considering intersectionality in fairness evaluation. Individual fairness scores are consistently worse than group fairness scores.
- Within-group unfairness is almost as high as individual unfairness and consistently higher than between-group unfairness.
- Generally, as group/individual Fair measures, Gini, Atk, and CV are fairer when NDCG is better.
- Including sensitive attributes in the prompt has little effect on effectiveness/fairness.
- Future work involves studying how strategies that mitigate either group or individual fairness affect each fairness type.

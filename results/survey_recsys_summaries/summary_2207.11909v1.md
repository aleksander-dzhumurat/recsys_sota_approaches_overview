### Main Idea
- This paper investigates the chemical abundances in the broad-line region (BLR) of the high-redshift quasar ULAS J1342+0928 at z = 7.54 to infer the properties of Population III (Pop III) stars.
- The motivation is to find evidence of Pop III stars, which are the first generation of stars formed from pristine gas, by analyzing the heavy element abundance patterns in high-redshift objects. The [Mg/Fe] abundance ratio is particularly useful for identifying the type of supernova (SN) that enriched the BLR gas.
- The study advances a novel flux-to-abundance conversion method to deduce the Fe and Mg abundances from the intensities of ultraviolet Mg II and Fe II emission lines in the near-infrared spectrum of the quasar. They propose that the unusual abundance pattern observed in ULAS J1342, with a very low Mg/Fe ratio and high Fe abundance, is best explained by enrichment from a pair-instability supernova (PISN) resulting from the explosion of a massive Pop III star.

### Technologies
- **Observational Data:** The near-infrared (NIR) spectrum of ULAS J1342+0928 at z = 7.54, obtained with the Gemini Near-InfraRed Spectrograph (GNIRS) at the Gemini North telescope. The spectrum covers observed wavelengths of 0.9-2.5 µm with a spectral resolution of R ~ 760.
- **Spectral Analysis:** Fitting of the observed spectrum with a power-law plus Balmer continuum and an Fe II template (Tsuzuki et al. 2006). Measurement of rest-frame equivalent widths (EWs) of Mg II λ2798 and Fe II emission lines. Estimation of black hole mass (MBH) and bolometric luminosity (Lbol) using standard methods.
- **Flux-to-Abundance Conversion Method:** A novel method developed by Sameshima et al. (2017, 2020) to convert the measured EWs of Mg II and Fe II emission lines to [Mg/Fe] and [Fe/H] abundance ratios, correcting for Eddington ratio dependence and Baldwin effect.
- **Chemical Evolution Models:** Comparison of the observed abundance ratios with standard and variant chemical evolution models for elliptical galaxies, considering contributions from core-collapse supernovae (CCSNe) and SNe Ia.
- **Nucleosynthesis Calculations:** Use of nucleosynthesis calculations from Takahashi et al. (2018) to determine the [Mg/Fe] abundance ratio as a function of progenitor mass for Pop III PISNe. Consideration of other nucleosynthesis calculations (Umeda & Nomoto 2002; Heger & Woosley 2002).
- **Statistical Analysis:**  Monte Carlo simulations were used to estimate errors based on measurement errors of the observables.  Fitting of SDSS quasar data to determine exponents for EW correction equations.
- **Software/Libraries:**  Utilized standard techniques and templates common in the research area.

### Results
- The BLR of ULAS J1342 is found to be extremely iron-enriched ([Fe / H] = +1.36 ± 0.19) with a very low Mg/Fe abundance ratio ([Mg / Fe] = -1.11 ± 0.12).
- Standard chemical evolution models for elliptical galaxies fail to reproduce the observed [Mg/Fe] and [Fe/H] values for ULAS J1342 at z = 7.54, suggesting that the heavy elements in the quasar BLR originate from a different source than the standard chemical evolution.
- The unusual abundance pattern is best explained by enrichment from a PISN caused by the explosion of a massive Pop III star with a mass near the high-mass end of the range of 150-300 M⊙, specifically around 280 M⊙.
- Alternative chemical evolution models, assuming initial PISN enrichment, can explain the trend in [Fe/Mg] from z < 3 to z = 7.54.
- The study predicts that stars with very low [Mg/Fe] at all metallicities are hidden in the Galaxy and could be discovered by ongoing photometric surveys.
- Distinguishing between a 280 M⊙ Pop III PISN and a 1000 M⊙ Pop III CCSN is possible by measuring [Si/Fe] using Si emission lines.  Also, other abundance ratios such as [Si/Mg] and [Al/Mg] are expected to show Pop III PISN characteristics.

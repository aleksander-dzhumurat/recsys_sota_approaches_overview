### Main Idea
- The paper addresses the challenge of reranking in multi-stage recommendation systems, which involves ordering a list of candidate items to maximize user engagement. The core problem is that existing autoregressive generative models used for this task are slow and prone to error accumulation, making them difficult to deploy in real-time industrial settings.
- The motivation stems from the need for efficient and effective reranking solutions that can handle the dynamic nature of user preferences and the vast permutation space of item sequences.
- The paper proposes NAR4Rec, a N on-A uto R egressive generative model for reranking R ecommendation, to generate the target item sequence simultaneously instead of one by one. To tackle challenges such as sparse training samples and dynamic candidates, they introduce a matching model. Also they employ a sequence-level unlikelihood training objective to differentiate feasible sequences from unfeasible ones and introduce contrastive decoding to capture correlations among these items.

### Technologies
- **Non-Autoregressive Generative Model:** NAR4Rec generates the entire item sequence in parallel, addressing the inference speed bottleneck of autoregressive models.
- **Matching Model:** A matching mechanism is used to address the dynamic vocabulary by matching each candidate item with each position in the target sequence. This mechanism consists of a candidates encoder using a Transformer architecture and a position encoder that also uses a Transformer architecture with cross-attention between the candidates and positions.
- **Unlikelihood Training:** A sequence-level unlikelihood training objective is used to differentiate between desirable and undesirable sequences, aligning the training process with user utility.
- **Contrastive Decoding:** Contrastive decoding is introduced to model dependencies among items and capture intra-list correlations, addressing the conditional independence assumption of non-autoregressive models.
- **Sequence Evaluator:** A self-attention based sequence evaluator estimates the overall utility of a given sequence and is used to pick the optimal sequence generated by the generator.
- **Datasets:** Experiments were conducted on the public Avito dataset and a large-scale industrial dataset from Kuaishou.
- **Metrics:** Evaluation metrics include AUC, LogLoss, and NDCG for the Avito dataset, and Recall@6, Recall@10, and LogLoss for the Kuaishou dataset.

### Results
- Offline experiments demonstrate that NAR4Rec outperforms state-of-the-art reranking methods, including DNN, DCN, PRM, Edge-Rerank, PIER, and Seq2Slate, on both the Avito and Kuaishou datasets.
- NAR4Rec achieves a significant speedup in both training and inference time compared to autoregressive models like Seq2Slate.
- Online A/B tests on the Kuaishou platform show that NAR4Rec significantly enhances user engagement, with improvements in views, likes, follows, long views, and complete views.
- Ablation studies validate the effectiveness of the unlikelihood training and contrastive decoding components of NAR4Rec.
- NAR4Rec has been successfully deployed in the Kuaishou video app, serving over 300 million daily active users, which shows real-world applicability and positive user experience.

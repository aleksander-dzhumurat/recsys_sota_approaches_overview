### Main Idea
- The paper addresses the lack of training-centric approaches for enhancing Large Language Models (LLMs) in Conversational Recommender Systems (CRSs). Existing LLM-based CRSs primarily focus on leveraging the summarization and analysis capabilities of LLMs through prompting techniques, while neglecting the potential improvements achievable through model-level training.
- The motivation is to enhance the intrinsic conversational recommendation abilities of LLMs at the model level, aiming to streamline CRSs by reducing reliance on complex pipelines and sophisticated prompts. The paper highlights the challenges associated with fine-tuning LLMs, such as overfitting and noise in training datasets, and the costly nature of Reinforcement Learning (RL) strategies that depend on manual scoring.
- The proposed solution is an integrated training-inference framework called User-Simulator-Based framework (USB-Rec). It uses an LLM-based Preference Optimization (PO) dataset construction strategy for RL training and a Self-Enhancement Strategy (SES) at the inference stage to further exploit the conversational recommendation potential obtained from RL training.

### Technologies
- **LLM-based Preference Optimization Dataset Construction:** This involves using an LLM-based user simulator to evaluate the responses generated by the recommender LLM. The simulator scores the responses, and these scores are used to construct a preference pair dataset for RL training. The preference pair dataset consists of preferred and dispreferred responses, determined through majority voting based on the user simulator's scores.
- **Reinforcement Learning (RL):** RL is used to train the LLM based on the preference pair dataset. The RL training aims to shift the output distribution of the LLM closer to the target, improving its ability to generate appropriate conversational recommendations.
- **Self-Enhancement Strategy (SES):** SES is applied at the inference stage to further refine the responses. It uses a user preference summarizer to create an internal user simulator by summarizing preceding dialogues. This internal user simulator then scores multiple sampled responses from the recommender LLM, and the optimal response is returned to the user.
- **LoRA (Low-Rank Adaptation):** LoRA is used during the SFT and PO stages to fine-tune the LLMs efficiently by reducing the number of trainable parameters.
- **SimPO (Simple Preference Optimization):** SimPO is used in the RL stage as the optimization algorithm.
- **Tree Search Strategy:** A tree search strategy combined with majority voting is used to mitigate bias in scoring due to the internal simulator's lack of awareness of true labels. This strategy iteratively samples multiple responses at each turn, branching into new simulated dialogues with the internal user simulator.
- **Datasets:** ReDial and OpenDialkg, two popular real-world conversational recommendation datasets, are used for experiments.
- **Evaluation Metrics:** iEval and Recall@1 are used to evaluate the performance of the proposed method. iEval is used to evaluate recommendation quality, while Recall@1 is used to measure the accuracy of item recommendations.

### Results
- The experiments show that the proposed USB-Rec method consistently outperforms previous state-of-the-art methods on various datasets.
- USB-Rec achieves the highest iEval scores on both ReDial (1.29) and OpenDialKG (1.40), indicating superior recommendation quality.
- In terms of Recall@1, USB-Rec attains competitive performance, getting the sub-optimal result on ReDial dataset, the same as UniCRS (0.050), and achieving a near-top score on OpenDialKG (0.300), closely following BARCOR (0.312) and UniCRS (0.308).
- A generalizability study demonstrates that the framework improves the recommendation ability of multiple LLMs, including Llama3.1-8B-instruct, ChatGLM3-6B, and Qwen2.5-7B-instruct. Even when fine-tuning alone doesn't show significant gains, the model learns the potential for conversational recommendation, which SES can then fully utilize.
- Ablation studies explore the impact of various hyperparameters within the SES, such as the search sampling temperature, majority voting, tree search depth, and tree search width, providing insights into optimal configurations. For example, a temperature of 0.5 for the recommender LLM during the internal search was found to be appropriate.
- A time-consuming comparison shows that the algorithm increased the metric value by 0.08 using only 3s more time when SES was applied only in the last round.

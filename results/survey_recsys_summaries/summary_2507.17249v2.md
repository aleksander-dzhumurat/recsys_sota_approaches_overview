### Main Idea

- The paper addresses the challenge of enhancing recommendation systems by leveraging Large Language Models (LLMs). Existing approaches, which rely on simple prompt techniques for knowledge acquisition, are susceptible to errors due to their dependence on System-1 thinking (fast, intuitive reasoning).
- The paper proposes R^4ec, a reasoning, reflection, and refinement framework designed to evolve the recommendation system into a weak System-2 model (slow, deliberate reasoning). This approach aims to mitigate errors in the reasoning process, leading to more accurate and reliable recommendations.
- The core idea is to incorporate two models: an actor model that performs reasoning and a reflection model that evaluates the actor's responses and provides feedback. The actor then refines its responses based on this feedback, iteratively improving the extracted knowledge. This refined knowledge is then integrated into a recommendation backbone for prediction.

### Technologies

- **Actor Model (𝜋 𝜃)**: A small LLM (Qwen-2.5 7B) responsible for reasoning about user preferences and item factual knowledge and refining this knowledge based on feedback.
- **Reflection Model (𝜋 𝜓)**: A small LLM (various Qwen-2.5 models ranging from 0.5B to 72B) tasked with judging the rationality of the actor model's output and providing reflections for outputs deemed unreasonable.
- **Iterative Reflection and Refinement Mechanism**: A process where the actor model generates knowledge, the reflection model provides feedback, and the actor refines its knowledge based on the feedback. This process continues iteratively until the reflection model deems the knowledge rational or a maximum number of iterations is reached.
- **LoRA (Low-Rank Adaptation)**: A parameter-efficient fine-tuning technique used to train the actor and reflection models, reducing computational demands while maintaining good performance. LoRA rank *r* is set to 8, LoRA alpha *𝛼* is set to 16, and LoRA dropout is set to 0.05. The LoRA update matrices are applied on all linear layers.
- **Knowledge Encoder (BGE-M3)**: A model used to transform the text-based knowledge generated by the actor and reflection models into dense vectors for integration into the recommendation backbone.
- **Recommendation Backbones**: Various classic CTR models (DIEN, GRU4Rec, AutoInt, FiGNN, DCN, DeepFM) are used as the base recommendation models to incorporate the refined knowledge.
- **Datasets**:
    - Amazon-Book: A dataset containing book reviews from Amazon.
    - MovieLens-1M: A movie recommendation dataset with user-movie ratings.
    - Industrial Dataset: A large-scale advertising platform dataset with hundreds of millions of users.
- **Evaluation Metrics**: AUC (Area Under the ROC Curve) and LogLoss (binary cross-entropy loss) are used to evaluate the performance of the models.
- **Inference Strategies**:
    - Iterative Refinement: The actor model generates knowledge, and the reflection model provides feedback for iterative refinement until the knowledge is deemed reasonable.
    - Reflection as a Filter: The actor model generates multiple knowledge samples, and the reflection model filters out unreasonable samples.

### Results

- R^4ec demonstrates significant improvements in recommendation performance compared to baseline models and existing LLM-enhanced methods like KAR.
- On Amazon-Book and MovieLens-1M datasets, R^4ec achieves higher AUC and lower LogLoss compared to baseline models, KAR, and R^2ec (a version of R^4ec without the reflection and refinement mechanisms). R^4ec achieves a 1.36% greater relative improvement in AUC and a 3.29% greater relative reduction in LogLoss than KAR. It also outperforms R^2ec, showing a 0.60% higher relative increase in AUC and a 1.93% greater relative reduction in LogLoss on the Amazon-Book dataset, and a 0.62% greater relative improvement in AUC and a 0.68% larger relative reduction in LogLoss on the MovieLens-1M dataset.
- Online A/B testing on a large-scale advertising platform shows a 2.2% increase in revenue and a 1.6% improvement in the conversion rate when using R^4ec. For long-tail data, R^4ec achieves a 4.1% increase in revenue and a 3.2% improvement in the conversion rate.
- Experiments investigating the scaling properties of the reflection model show that increasing the size of the reflection model leads to consistent improvements in AUC and reductions in LogLoss, up to the largest model tested (72B).
- Scaling the actor model also improves performance, but the marginal benefit of the reflection model diminishes as the actor model becomes larger.
- The iterative refinement inference strategy consistently outperforms the reflection-as-a-filter strategy, suggesting that refinement is crucial for correcting unreasonable knowledge.
- Increasing the number of iterative refinement steps leads to continuous improvements in AUC and reductions in LogLoss, but the rate of improvement diminishes with each iteration.

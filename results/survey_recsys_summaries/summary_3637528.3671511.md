### Main Idea

- **Primary Research Objective:** The paper addresses the problem of live streaming gift prediction, which is crucial for enhancing user experience and streamer revenue. The core challenge is accurately modeling user preferences in the context of rapidly changing live content and sparse gifting behaviors.
- **Motivation and Significance:** Existing methods either ignore the dynamic content changes in live streaming or struggle with the sparsity of gifting behaviors, leading to suboptimal recommendation effectiveness. Improving gifting prediction directly translates to increased user engagement and platform revenue, making it a high-impact area.
- **Proposed Approach:** MMBee is proposed based on real-time Multi-Modal Fusion and Behavior Expansion. It fuses multi-modal content (visual frames, comments, speech) to capture real-time content changes and expands user behavior sequences using a graph-guided interest expansion approach to alleviate sparsity.

### Technologies

- **Multi-modal Fusion Module with Learnable Query (MFQ):** This module processes the visual frames, comments, and speech in each streaming segment. Orthogonal Projection (OP) operation maximizes the complementation effects between different modalities. Learnable query tokens extract streamer-aware content patterns.
- **Graph-guided Interest Expansion (GIE):** This approach constructs large-scale gifting graphs (User-to-Author and Author-to-Author) to capture relationships between users and streamers.
    - **Graph Node Representations Pre-training:** Uses graph contrastive learning (GraphCL) to learn general user and streamer embeddings.
    - **Metapath-based Behavior Expansion:** Expands behavior sequences using metapaths to explore potential preferences.
- **Underlying Recommendation Model:** SIM (Search-based Interest Model) is used as the foundational model due to its widespread usage, online efficiency, and effectiveness.
- **Datasets:**
    - Kuaishou Dataset: 3 billion user interaction logs with live-streaming content.
    - Public Datasets: TikTok and MovieLens for validating the MFQ and GIE module's effectiveness.
- **Evaluation Metrics:** AUC, UAUC (User Average AUC), GAUC (Group Average AUC), Recall@10, Precision@10, and NDCG@10.
- **Experimental Setup:** Offline experiments on Kuaishou and public datasets. Online A/B tests on Kuaishou's live streaming platform.

### Results

- **Performance on Kuaishou Dataset:** MMBee significantly outperforms baseline models like MMoE and SIM. It achieves notable improvements in AUC, UAUC, and GAUC metrics, indicating better ranking capability and user-specific performance. For example, SIM+Ours(MFQ+GIE) achieves +0.1646% AUC, +1.1439% UAUC and +1.2057 % GAUC improvements over SIM.
- **Performance on Public Datasets (TikTok and MovieLens):** MMBee shows competitive results compared to state-of-the-art graph-based and multi-modal recommendation models, demonstrating generalizability beyond the live streaming domain.
- **Ablation Studies:** Ablation studies confirm the importance of both the MFQ and GIE modules. Removing either module leads to significant performance drops. Analysis of the multi-modal features indicates that visual modality has the most important impact.
- **Online A/B Testing:** Online A/B tests on Kuaishou's live streaming platform demonstrate a 2.862% increase in NGU (Number of users who sent gifts) and a 4.775% lift in NGC (Total number of gifts sent).
- **Inference Time:** An offline storage strategy is applied to pre-request expansion behaviors. The online recommendation model could access the embedding server instead of walking through the graph on the fly. The response time of MMBee is only about 1 ms more than that of the baseline system on average.

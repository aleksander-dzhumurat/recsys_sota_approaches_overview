### Main Idea
- **Problem:** The paper addresses the Item Cold-Start (ICS) problem in recommender systems, specifically in zero-shot scenarios where new items have no prior interaction data. Existing methods that learn globally shared prior knowledge struggle with items lacking any interactions.
- **Motivation:** Frequent updates of products in platforms like Alipay exacerbate the ICS problem, hindering the ability to recommend new items effectively. Traditional recommender systems rely on user-item interaction data, which is absent for cold items, leading to degraded performance. Efficiency and timeliness are also crucial for large-scale recommender systems.
- **Solution:** The paper proposes an Industrial Framework for Item Cold Start (Indics System), deployed on Alipay, to provide efficient and high-quality recommendations for cold items without any log data. It formulates the ICS problem as a zero-shot learning problem. It decomposes the recommendation process into two stages: an offline stage for generating cold item representations and an online stage leveraging a lightweight ICS model. A key component is the Latent Embedding Predictor (LEP), which maps item attributes to a semantic space, connecting cold and warm items.

### Technologies
- **Framework Architecture:** Hybrid offline-online architecture consisting of Metadata, Latent Model, Latent Embedding Predictor (LEP), and Cold-Start Model.
    - **Metadata:** Collects user-item interactions from historical campaigns and provides item attributes like business category, target groups, and discounts.
    - **Latent Model:** Learns a preference embedding space representing items based on Metadata (e.g., collaborative filtering).
    - **Latent Embedding Predictor (LEP):** Maps item attributes to the latent semantic embedding learned by the Latent Model. Any common net architecture can be used for LEP. The training strategy is to make the predicted latent embedding of LEP to be similar with the real latent embedding learned by Latent Model . The similarity is constrained by distance metrics (e.g. cosine similarity).
    - **Cold-Start Model:** Generates user-item preference predictions using the item latent embedding (predicted by LEP) and user attributes. Collaborative Filtering (CF) and Deep Neural Network (DNN) can be used as the architecture of Cold-Start Model.
- **Zero-Shot Learning Formulation:** Formulates the cold-start problem as mapping item attribute space to a semantic space.
- **Evaluation Metrics:** Accuracy, AUC, and RelaImp (Relative Improvement) for offline evaluation. Conversion Rate (CVR) for online A/B testing.
- **Baselines:** ItemPop (popularity-based heuristic), MLP (Multi-Layer Perceptron), Dropout-Net, Meta-Embedding Generator, and MELU.
- **Visualization:** t-SNE is employed to visualize the latent embedding space.

### Results
- **Offline Evaluation:** The Indics System consistently outperforms baseline methods, demonstrating the necessity of addressing ICS in zero-shot scenarios. It achieves better Accuracy, AUC, and RelaImp compared to ItemPop, MLP, Dropout-Net, Emb-Gen, and MELU.
- **Online A/B Testing:** Deployed in the "Five Fortune Card Collection" campaign on Alipay, the Indics System increased online Conversion Rate (CVR) by 15.3% compared to the default method.
- **Latent Embedding Visualization:** t-SNE visualization shows that items in the same business and category share nearby latent embedding spaces. Cold items similar to warm items are also close in the latent space, indicating effective integration.
- **Interpretable Recommendation:** The Indics System provides high-quality personalized recommendations by recommending cold items to users who purchased similar warm items, addressing the limitations of popularity-based and standard MLP approaches.
- **Efficiency:** The Indics System demonstrates remarkable computational efficiency, consuming less memory and time compared to meta-learning-based methods like MELU and Meta-Embedding Generator.

### Main Idea
- The paper reviews the use of pulsating stars (RR Lyrae, Anomalous Cepheids, and Classical Cepheids) in Local Group (LG) dwarf galaxies to infer properties of their host galaxies. Pulsating stars, acting as standard candles, are crucial for determining distances, ages, metallicity gradients, and star formation histories of these galaxies. The presence and type of pulsating stars provide insights into the stellar populations and formation histories of the dwarf galaxies. This review highlights recent discoveries and ongoing research in the field, emphasizing the importance of these stars in understanding the structure and evolution of the Local Group. It further discusses the potential of using RRL period-luminosity relations with data from upcoming surveys like the Vera C. Rubin LSST.

### Technologies
- **Data Sources**: Surveys like ASAS, Catalina, DES, Gaia, OGLE, PanSTARRS, ZTF, Dark Energy Camera (DES), Hyper Suprime Cam, Pan-STARRS, Gaia, and Sloan Digital Sky Survey (SDSS) are used for detecting and analyzing pulsating stars.
- **Stellar Population Analysis**: Color-magnitude diagrams (CMDs) are used to differentiate stellar populations and identify pulsating stars.
- **Distance Determination**: Period-luminosity relations (Henrietta Leavitt's Law) are applied to derive accurate and precise distances to dwarf galaxies.
- **Metallicity Tracers**: RR Lyrae stars are utilized as metallicity tracers to infer the chemical evolution of old stellar populations.
- **Statistical Analysis**: Statistical methods are used to correlate the frequency of Anomalous Cepheids with the absolute V magnitude of the host galaxy.
- **Tidal Disruption Analysis**: Spatial distribution of RR Lyrae stars is used to detect tidal stripping and assess the extent of dwarf galaxies.
- **Photometric Systems**: SDSS pass-bands (ugrizy) are used for observations and building period-luminosity relations.
- **Theoretical Models**: Evolutionary tracks for stars igniting helium in degenerate and non-degenerate conditions are used to compare with observed Cepheids.
- **Software/Libraries**: Gaia DR2 RRL catalog (Clementini et al. 2019).

### Results
- RR Lyrae stars are the most common pulsating stars in LG dwarf galaxies, serving as tracers of ancient stellar populations and tools for detecting new low-mass dwarf galaxies.
- Classical Cepheids indicate young stellar populations, while Anomalous Cepheids can indicate old or intermediate-age populations, depending on their origin.
- The detection of RR Lyrae stars in ultra-faint dwarf (UFD) galaxies provides precise distance measurements, improving estimates of absolute visual magnitude and physical size.
- The number of RR Lyrae stars correlates with the absolute V magnitude of the host galaxy (log NRRL = -0.296 ± 0.013 MV - 0.797 ± 0.107), indicating that RRL-based searches are effective for UFDs brighter than MV ∼ -4 mag.
- Tidal disruption events can be inferred from the spatial distribution of RR Lyrae stars, such as in Hercules, Crater II, and Antlia II.
- Empirical period-luminosity relations in SDSS bands (g, r, i) were derived for galaxies like Sextans, Crater II, and Eridanus II, showing metallicity-dependent zero-point differences.
- Future surveys like Vera C. Rubin LSST will provide more data for homogeneous distance measurements using RR Lyrae stars, enhancing our understanding of Galactic structure.

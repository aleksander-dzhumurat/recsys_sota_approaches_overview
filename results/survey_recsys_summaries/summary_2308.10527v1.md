### Main Idea
- The paper addresses the problem of predicting Click-Through Rate (CTR) in relevant recommendation scenarios within e-commerce platforms.  Relevant recommendations suggest related items to a trigger item that a user is interested in.
- The key challenges are to learn fine-grained representations of similarity and diversity among items and to capture users' dynamic preferences for these aspects under different conditions. Existing approaches often overlook users' changing preferences and fail to model diversity at a fine-grained level.
- The paper proposes a Dynamic Preference-based and Attribute-aware Network (DPAN) to address these challenges. DPAN leverages rich attribute information, learns similarity and diversity representations, and captures dynamic user preferences based on various conditions to improve CTR.

### Technologies
- **Attribute-aware Activation Values Generation (AAVG)**: Used to generate activation values for each attribute sequence based on rich attribute information (e.g., category, brand, price, and title).  It leverages the trigger and target items' attributes to activate user behavior sequences.
- **Bi-dimensional Compression-based Re-expression (BCR)**: Applied after AAVG to obtain fine-grained similarity and diversity representations of user interests and item information. It compresses information along the attribute dimension in User Interests Embedding Module (UIEM) and along the sequence dimension in Item Information Embedding Module (IIEM).
- **Shallow and Deep Union-based Fusion (SDUF)**: Proposed to capture users' dynamic preferences for the diverse degree of recommendation results according to various conditions. It includes a shallow union network and a deep union network, whose parameters are dynamically generated by a parameter generation network based on conditional information (e.g., context and trigger information).
- **Feature Embedding Layer**:  Transforms input features (trigger item, target item, user behavior sequence, user profile, and context information) into embeddings.
- **Multi-Layer Perceptron (MLP)**: Used to aggregate the similarity and diversity representations and for the final scoring network to predict click probability.
- **Dataset**:  A proprietary industrial dataset collected from the platform's relevant recommendation scenario.  The dataset includes user interactions, trigger items, target items, and contextual information.
- **Evaluation Metric**: Area Under the Curve (AUC) and RelaImpr (relative improvements) are used to evaluate the performance of the models. Logloss is used for training.

### Results
- DPAN demonstrated superior effectiveness compared to state-of-the-art (SOTA) models in offline experiments. DPAN achieved a **10.69% relative improvement in AUC** compared to the baseline DIN model.
- Online A/B testing showed a significant **7.62% increase in CTR** when using DPAN compared to DIN.
- Ablation studies revealed the importance of each component of DPAN: the auxiliary loss in AAVG, the use of multiple attributes, the similarity representations in BCR, and the union networks in SDUF.
- The model's ability to capture users' preferences for similarity and diversity was analyzed by examining the exposed category and brand numbers per user under different channels (GUL vs. SRP). DPAN provided items with a wider range of categories and brands when users navigated from GUL, aligning with expectations that users from GUL prefer diversity.
